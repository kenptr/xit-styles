Creativity is like a muscle — you need to train it to get stronger.

Congratulations on using SparcIt to discover your Creativity Quotient (CQ)!

With more than 50 years of psychological research, this report will help you understand the results of the CQ assessment. The CQ assessment is designed to measure an individual’s creativity and innovativeness. Creativity and innovation are a combination of insights, originality, imagination, openness, and action. This report provides information that will help you recognize your overall creativity level and the individual components that comprise it. The report will also provide you with information that helps you understand and manage your strengths and weaknesses across the four key areas of creativity: Originality, Flexibility, Fluency and Elaboration.

  • ORIGINALITY is how unique and "out-of-this-world" your solutions are.
  • FLEXIBILITY is your ability to look at a problem from different perspectives. In other words, it’s the ability to look at a problem from different angles or points of view.
  • FLUENCY is how many unique and relevant responses you can come up with in a given time.
  • ELABORATION is your ability to expand on an idea by embellishing it with details.

The information provided in this report will allow you to better understand both your overall creativity level and its individual components. Use this opportunity to also learn how to manage your strengths and weaknesses in the four key areas of the CQ. Remember, creativity is a skill that is improved with practice. The more you work on it, the better you will get.

Development tips

Originality is about creating novel ideas. Improve your Originality by thinking of out-of-the-box ideas or solutions. Look for sources of inspiration to help you get out of your “comfort zone”. Sources of inspiration can be anything, including nature, books, museums, music, work, or a debate with a friend. Generate ideas and write as if you are writing to a friend. There is no judgment. Create “what if” approaches to analyze different scenarios. Then, see if and how the generated idea or solution can be applied to improve an everyday problem for you. Keep practicing. The more you do this, the more likely you are to create a novel idea.

Improve your Flexibility by thinking in a non-linear way. Find a problem and look at it from different angles. One way of doing this is to use the “Superhero” technique. Individually or with your team, name your top five favorite superheroes. Then, pick one superhero at a time and pretend you are him/her. Put yourself in the superhero’s position, and think about what s/he would do about the problem.

Another method is Edward De Bono’s “six hats” technique. The “six hats” technique involves looking at a problem from six different perspectives. By doing this, you can produce more ideas than you would if you only looked at the situation from one or two points of view.

Don’t be embarrassed about “out there” solutions. You might surprise yourself and produce something unique and novel.

Your Fluency score wasn’t high, because you didn’t generate a sufficient number of ideas. Research shows that the first third of ideas generated are ones that have already been tried; the second third are ideas that have previously been considered; and the final third is where breakthrough ideas are generated. Stop editing yourself. Practice divergent thinking, where you learn to state ideas without judging them. Also, practice ways to build upon the great foundation you have when it comes to thinking up ideas. For instance, get a blank sheet of paper and write down a list of all the things you can think of when you turn over a flash card with an image on it. Push yourself to think of things that may seem a bit crazy or unconventional. Fluency is all about the flow of ideas and training your mind to be free so you can think of as many things as possible without constriction. Free yourself of the mindset that all of your ideas have to make sense.

You provided ideas and possible solutions, but you didn’t necessarily provide enough details. This affected your Elaboration score. You might be a big picture person, but sometimes, your peers and colleagues will need more details from you. Give them the details they need by elaborating and supporting your point of view. A great exercise to improve your Elaboration is a mini-saga. Wherever you are, choose two objects close to you. Then think about a story or a scenario that you can create around these two objects. This will help you improve your Elaboration.
